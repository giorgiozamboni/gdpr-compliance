## 1. Systematic description of the processing (Art. 35(7)(a))
- What data, from whom, how, with what systems, and the data flows.
- Purposes (and the legitimate interest, if that is the basis).
- Scope, context, retention, recipients, transfers.

## 2. Necessity and proportionality (Art. 35(7)(b))
- Is the processing **necessary** for the purpose, or could a less intrusive means achieve it?
- Lawful basis, data minimisation, accuracy, storage limitation, transparency, data-subject rights.

## 3. Risks to rights and freedoms (Art. 35(7)(c))
For each risk: source, harm scenario, likelihood, severity, overall risk level.
- Risk 1: ____________________
- Risk 2: ____________________
- Consider: unauthorised access, unlawful use, loss of control, discrimination/bias, re-identification.

## 4. Measures to address the risks (Art. 35(7)(d))
- Safeguards, security measures (Art. 32), pseudonymisation/encryption, access controls.
- Organisational measures, transparency, mechanisms for data-subject rights.
- **Residual risk** after mitigation: low / medium / **high**.
