# Records of processing (ROPA, Art. 30) and accountability

> Accountability (Art. 5(2)) means you must **demonstrate** compliance, not just achieve it. The core
> evidence is the **Records of Processing Activities (ROPA)**. **Verify against Arts. 5, 24, 30 EUR-Lex and
> Garante guidance.**

## The accountability principle (Arts. 5(2), 24)
You must implement appropriate technical and organisational measures **and be able to show** they are
appropriate: policies, records, DPIAs, DPAs, training, and a paper trail of decisions. "We are careful" is
not accountability; the documentation is.

## Records of Processing Activities (Art. 30)
A structured inventory of your processing. Controllers record, per processing activity:
- Controller (and DPO/representative) identity and contacts.
- **Purposes** of the processing.
- **Categories of data subjects** and **categories of personal data**.
- **Categories of recipients** (incl. those in third countries).
- **International transfers** and the safeguards used.
- **Retention** periods (time limits for erasure) where possible.
- A general description of the **security measures** (Art. 32) where possible.

Processors keep a lighter record (Art. 30(2)): controllers they act for, categories of processing,
transfers, security.

⚠ The "**under 250 employees**" exemption is **narrow**: it does **not** apply if the processing is not
occasional, is likely to result in a risk, or involves special-category/criminal data. In practice most
SMEs that process customer/employee data regularly **must keep a ROPA**. Do not tell a client they are
exempt from memory. Use `assets/ropa-template.md`.

## Do you need a DPO? (Arts. 37-39)
Mandatory if you are a public authority, or your **core activities** involve **regular and systematic
monitoring on a large scale**, or **large-scale** special-category/criminal-data processing. Otherwise
optional but often useful. ⛔ The "large scale / core activity" call is a judgment: name it.

## Data protection by design and by default (Art. 25)
Build in data minimisation, purpose limitation, and privacy defaults from the start (not bolted on). This
is the seam with the AI Act's data-governance duties and with the `security-audit` skill.

## Security of processing (Art. 32)
Appropriate measures to the risk: pseudonymisation/encryption where apt, confidentiality, integrity,
availability, resilience, and regular testing. For the concrete controls, use the `security-audit` skill.
