# Data-subject rights and how to handle requests

> People have enforceable rights over their data (Arts. 12-22). Mishandling a request (ignoring it, missing
> the deadline, over-disclosing) is itself a breach of duty. **Verify articles and current EDPB guidance;
> verify deadlines rather than stating them from memory.**

## The rights (Arts. 15-22)
- **Information (Arts. 13-14)** — the privacy notice, at collection.
- **Access (Art. 15)** — a copy of their data + the required information.
- **Rectification (Art. 16)** — correct inaccurate/incomplete data.
- **Erasure / "right to be forgotten" (Art. 17)** — in defined cases (no longer necessary, consent
  withdrawn, unlawful processing), subject to exceptions (legal obligation, freedom of expression, claims).
- **Restriction (Art. 18)** — pause processing while a dispute is resolved.
- **Portability (Art. 20)** — receive/transmit data provided, in a machine-readable format, where
  processing is by consent or contract and automated.
- **Objection (Art. 21)** — object to processing based on legitimate interests or public task; **absolute**
  right to object to **direct marketing**.
- **Automated decisions / profiling (Art. 22)** — right not to be subject to a solely automated decision
  with legal/significant effect, with exceptions (contract, law, explicit consent) that require safeguards
  incl. **human intervention**. This is the key **AI Act seam** (hand off to `ai-act-compliance`).

## Handling a request (the workflow)
1. **Recognise it.** A request can arrive by any channel, in any words. Log it on receipt (the clock
   starts).
2. **Verify identity** proportionately (do not use it to stall).
3. **Deadline:** respond **without undue delay and within one month** of receipt, extendable by **two
   further months** for complex/numerous requests (inform the person within the first month). ⚠ Verify the
   current deadline before quoting it.
4. **Scope and retrieve** the data across all systems (including backups, logs, third-party processors).
5. **Redact third-party data** and anything covered by an exemption; do not over-disclose.
6. **Respond** in a concise, intelligible form; usually **free of charge** (a reasonable fee only for
   manifestly unfounded/excessive or repeat requests).
7. **Record** what you did.

## Practical SME setup
- A single intake point (privacy@ or a form), a logging sheet, an identity-check step, a retrieval
  checklist per system, and a template response. Handle marketing objections immediately and absolutely.

## Confidence
- The **rights and the one-month/two-month structure** are ✓ **Settled** (verify the exact deadline text).
- **Whether an exemption applies** (e.g. refusing erasure, redacting) is often ⚠/⛔: route hard refusals to
  the DPO/counsel.
